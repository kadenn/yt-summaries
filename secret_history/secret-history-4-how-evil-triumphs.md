## Thought experiment: “Monkey Island” (how elite cohesion forms)

- Scenario: 100 poor, uneducated men from different cultures/languages are stranded on an island with lethal “flesh-eating monkeys”; resources require constant fighting.
- Predicted group evolution under existential threat:

  - Rapid creation of a **common language**
  - Shared stories → a **founding myth**
  - Emergence of a **new religion** (“God chose us”)
  - Development of **rituals** to reinforce bonding (including sexual rituals)
  - Selection of leaders based on **demonstrated sacrifice**, not intelligence

- Leader-selection parable: a young man cuts off his hand without flinching; group chooses him because he signals **maximum commitment**.
- Outcome: extreme **cohesion** (brotherhood) leads to **synchronicity** (acting as one), framed as “hive mind” and even “telepathy.”
- Examples of sacrifice under synchronicity:

  - Cutting the rope bridge to save the group
  - Soldiers jumping on grenades in trench warfare

- Final step: when returned to the real world, this bonded group becomes a **“secret elite”** whose descendants inherit power; visible public leaders become **fronts/puppets**.
- Mentions extreme “funeral rituals” (including cannibalism) as possible cohesion-maintenance practices.

**Takeaway:** The speaker’s model treats **shared trauma + ritual + sacrifice** as a factory for **elite-level coordination** that can later dominate society.

## Historical analogies used to support the cohesion-through-transgression model

### Sparta

- Described as producing extreme military cohesion via:

  - Early-life separation and brutal “hazing”
  - Older soldier mentorship that includes sexual relationships (speaker claims it builds group solidarity)
  - “Graduation” rite: young soldiers kill helots at night (framed as sacrifice)

- Claim: Sparta was hated by others but remained unified and militarily dominant.

### Thebes (“Sacred Band”) and Macedon

- Thebes copies Spartan bonding model voluntarily; forms the **Sacred Band** of 300 paired lovers; becomes a dominant force after Sparta.
- Macedon (Philip II; later Alexander) copies/adapts these systems and defeats Thebes/Athens (Battle of Chaeronea, 338 BCE).
- Sacred Band’s last stand is presented as idealized commitment to honor/sacrifice.

**Takeaway:** Speaker uses these cases to argue that **taboo/violence/sexual bonding + shared rites** can produce unusually effective collective action.

**Takeaway:** Power comes from **cooperating covertly** while appearing uncoordinated.

## Metaphysical/philosophical justification (Kant → Hegel → Gnosticism/Plato/Dante)

### Kant (as presented)

- We don’t perceive objective reality directly; the mind filters reality into phenomena.
- Raises three questions:

  1. What is the noumenal reality?
  2. Where do the mind’s filters come from?
  3. How do we know we perceive the same world?

### Hegel (as presented)

- Answers with **“Geist” (spirit)**: spirit is the true underlying reality that generates the material world.
- Claims “geist” explains why minds align and why perception is shared.

### Gnosticism/Plato cosmology (as presented)

- Ultimate source is the **Monad/The One** (supreme divine origin).
- Reality is multi-layered; Earth is an “outer shell.”
- Purpose of life is to **return to the Monad**.

### Plato vs Dante: how to return

- **Plato:** return through **knowledge** (even “sacred geometry” / mathematics).
- **Dante:** return through **love**; love is accessible to everyone, unlike elite knowledge.

**Takeaway:** Speaker builds a spiritual cosmology where “good” = moving toward the Monad (love/knowledge), “evil” = moving away into materialism.
