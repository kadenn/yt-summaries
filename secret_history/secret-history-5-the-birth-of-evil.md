## 1. Core Thesis of the Lecture

- **Secret societies originate as underground preservers of pre-Christian religious worldviews**, especially those suppressed by monotheism.
- Their core belief: **mind precedes matter**, and modern religion (Christianity) inverted this truth.
- What modern society calls “evil” is framed as a **byproduct of monotheism**, not a universal moral truth.
- Christianity is presented not as spiritual liberation, but as **imperial control theology**.
- The lecture blends **anthropology, mythology, Gnosticism, and conspiracy-style metaphysics** into a single explanatory narrative.

---

## 2. Three Stages of Religious Development (Western World)

### 1) Mother Goddess Civilization (Pre-war, Pre-property)

**Origins**

- Emerges with agriculture.
- Two existential needs:

  - Crop fertility
  - Human reproduction

**Key Beliefs**

- The **womb** is sacred — a portal from the spiritual world.
- Women hold social and spiritual authority.
- Nature is **harmonized**: stars, seasons, fertility, sex.
- Astrology governs planting and reproduction cycles.

**Social Structure**

- No private property.
- No hierarchy.
- Sex is communal (fertility cults).
- Paternity is irrelevant; genetic “best outcome” prioritized.

**Symbols**

- Bird → dominion of the sky / source of life.
- Bull → male fertility and generative power.

**Worldview**

- Intuitive, holistic, metaphorical.
- Mind → matter.

---

### 2) Polytheism (War, Property, Hierarchy)

**Trigger**

- Population growth → resource scarcity → war.

**Key Shift**

- Women become property.
- Men fight wars in exchange for wives and land.
- Patriarchy emerges.

**Religion**

- Each tribe has its god.
- Wars = gods fighting each other.
- Losing a war = your god is weaker.
- Pantheons form (e.g., Zeus absorbs rival gods).

**Cosmology**

- Gods are immortal but human-like.
- Above gods exist abstract forces:

  - Fate
  - Fortune
  - Justice (cosmic law)

**Moral Framework**

- Not about being “good”
- About being **fortunate** (favored by gods)

---

### 3) Monotheism (Empire, Absolutism)

**Trigger**

- Rise of empires (especially Rome).
- Empires can annihilate entire populations → no need for religious pluralism.

**Key Shift**

- One true God
- One truth
- Universal obedience enforced

**Three New Dominant Concepts**

1. **Truth** (singular, absolute)
2. **Evil** (deviation from truth)
3. **The Individual** (faith overrides community)

**Consequences**

- Moral absolutism
- Suppression of alternative worldviews
- Destruction of metaphorical thinking
- Religion becomes **counterintuitive** (requires enforcement)

## 3. Metaphysical Shift: How Humans Understand Reality

- Matter creates mind.

**Claim**
